# Operator Discovery Methodology
## How Circuit 40s Researches Logistics Operators Before Building

**Published by:** Faruk Olawale Quadri, Co-Founder and COO, Circuit 40s Ltd
**Last updated:** May 2026

---

## Overview

This document outlines the research methodology Circuit 40s uses to understand how logistics operators work in practice before making product decisions. It is published here to contribute to the broader conversation about how freight technology companies can build more effectively by grounding product development in field research rather than assumptions.

The core principle behind this methodology is straightforward: the people executing freight on the ground know things that no amount of market research or desk analysis can surface. Before building anything, you have to go and ask them.

---

## Why Operator Discovery Matters

Most logistics technology is designed top-down. A team identifies a market opportunity, maps out a workflow based on their understanding of the industry, builds a product, and then asks operators to adopt it. When adoption is slow or the product does not work in practice, the problem is usually attributed to user behaviour or change resistance.

The real problem is almost always simpler. The product was built on assumptions that were never tested against reality.

Operator discovery is the process of replacing those assumptions with direct evidence. It involves speaking to the people who execute freight every day, in the markets and trade lanes you are building for, and understanding their actual workflows before a single product decision is made.

---

## Research Objectives

The goal of operator discovery at Circuit 40s is to answer the following questions:

- How does a logistics operator receive a new job today?
- What information do they need before they can commit to a shipment?
- How do they manage their stage of a shipment while it is active?
- What information passes between operators at each stage transition?
- How do operators confirm that their stage is complete?
- Where does the current process break down and why?

These questions are intentionally open-ended. The methodology is designed to surface operator reality, not to validate a pre-existing product hypothesis.

---

## Operator Selection Criteria

Operators are selected for discovery research based on the following criteria:

**Geographic diversity**
Operators are selected across multiple regions to capture how freight coordination differs by market, regulatory environment, and infrastructure. A cross-border road freight operator in East Africa faces different realities than a customs brokerage at a major European port. Both perspectives are necessary.

**Service type diversity**
Freight does not move through one type of operator. A single international shipment may pass through a freight forwarder, a customs agent, a port handling company, a trucking operator, and a last-mile delivery provider. Discovery research must cover all operator types to build a complete picture of how a shipment moves end to end.

**Scale diversity**
Operators range from independent sole traders handling shipments on a quotation basis to regional companies managing hundreds of shipments per month. Both ends of this spectrum are included. Scale affects how an operator communicates, what systems they use, and what friction points they experience.

**Verification status**
All operators included in discovery research are verified members of the Circuit 40s network. Verification involves confirming company registration, operational licenses, service capabilities, and geographic coverage before any research engagement begins.

## Research Format

Discovery research is conducted using two formats depending on operator availability and preference.

**Structured interview (preferred)**
A 20 to 30 minute call in which the operator is asked to walk through a real recent shipment from the moment they heard about it to the moment it left their hands. Questions are asked throughout to clarify specific stages, information flows, and handoff processes. Interviews are not recorded without consent. Notes are taken in real time and reviewed immediately after the call.

**Written questionnaire**
For operators unable to commit to a call, a written questionnaire is provided covering the same core topics as the interview. Questionnaire responses are reviewed alongside interview data to identify patterns and divergences.

---

## Question Design Principles

Questions are designed to surface operational reality rather than operator opinion about what a product should do. The distinction matters because operators are experts in their own workflow but not necessarily in product design.

Questions follow these principles:

**Open before specific**
Every session begins with an open invitation: walk us through your last shipment, start to finish. Only after the operator has described their process in their own words are specific clarifying questions introduced.

**Process before pain points**
Understanding how something works before asking what is wrong with it produces more accurate responses. Operators asked directly about pain points often describe the most recent frustration rather than the most structurally significant problem.

**No leading questions**
Questions do not suggest answers or reference how the product currently works. The goal is to hear the operator's version of reality before comparing it to any existing design.

**Specificity over generality**
Questions ask about the last shipment, not a typical shipment. Asking about a specific real event produces more accurate and detailed responses than asking about general patterns.

---

## Data Handling

All operator responses are treated as confidential commercial information. The following principles govern how discovery data is handled:

- Operator names and company details are never published in research outputs
- Specific responses are paraphrased or anonymised before use in any documentation
- Raw response data is stored securely and accessed only by the Circuit 40s product team
- No operator response is used in marketing or public-facing content without explicit consent

---

## How Findings Feed Into Product Development

Discovery findings are reviewed as a complete dataset rather than as individual responses. Patterns that appear across multiple operators in different markets are treated as structurally significant. Findings that appear in only one response are noted but not acted on without further validation.

Findings are used to answer three questions before any product decision is made:

1. Does the current product design reflect how operators actually work at this stage?
2. If not, what needs to change?
3. Are there stages or workflows we have not accounted for at all?

This process is ongoing. As the operator network grows and new markets are added, discovery research continues to validate and refine product decisions.
